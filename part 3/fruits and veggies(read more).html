Banana

*The average diet contains 6 to 8 bananas. ...
The banana is between 15 and 25 cm (6 and 9 in.) long and its green color indicates that it is not yet ripe. Bananas are at their best when their skin turns yellow and becomes dotted with dark spots.

Apple

The Red Delicious apple, dark red in color, has a crunchy yellow flesh that softens when cooked. ... It can be enjoyed as is, as a snack with peanut butter or sliced in a salad with grilled nuts.

Orange

Navel orange has a bright orange peel that is easy to peel and a sweet seedless pulp. ... This popular orange, champion of snacks, is also used to lacquer chicken, duck and pork.

Lettuce

The iceberg lettuce roundlet, or head lettuce, consists of densely grouped pale leaves with a discreet taste. ... It is mostly used fresh because of its crispy texture. Grate it to include it in a green or cabbage salad, unless you cut it into fleshy quarters and season with a blue cheese vinaigrette.

Tomato

Bright red when ripe, the greenhouse tomato has a firm, fleshy flesh that predestines it to garnish burgers or a traditional BLT sandwich. ... It can also be hollowed out and used for a stuffed tomato recipe.

Cucumber

The English (or greenhouse) cucumber is a cylindrical fruit with green skin and crunchy white flesh sprinkled with edible seeds. ... It is usually eaten raw, with or without the skin. Add a few slices to a vegetable platter or enjoy it in a salad with mint and lemon juice.
