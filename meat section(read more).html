Bacon

Our Bacon is is Certified Humane® (animal welfare) and comes from pork raised without antibiotics ... It is naturally smoked and contains no added nitrites or nitrates. The meat is salted and then naturally smoked. During smoking, all of the liquid from our brining process is evaporated, resulting in meaty, tender bacon that shrinks less when cooked. Add it to a grilled cheese sandwich, wrap it around a filet mignon, crumble it into cheese cookie buns or, to wow your dinner guests, infuse it with bourbon for an aperitif.

Beef

The prime rib roast is the creme de la creme of roasts ... Recognized for being tender and juicy, accompany it with bran au jus for even more flavor. We suggest baking at 325°F or if you have more time, at 275°F. The roast will be very juicy, provided it does not exceed the internal temperature of 71°C (160°F), which is equivalent to a “medium” degree of doneness. (Internal temperature for a medium-rare: 63°C or 145°F.) To make your roast even better, let it rest for about twenty minutes in aluminum foil after cooking. This last step will make all the difference.

Chicken

Top quality organic chickens. Chickens are air-chilled and vacuum-packed to extend shelf life and preserve flavor ... Available whole or in parts, chicken is appreciated for its tasty white meat that can be prepared in a thousand and one ways. A whole roast chicken, served with vegetables and potatoes, is a classic meal that fills all stomachs.

Lobster

Often caught around Prince Edward Island and the Magdalen Islands in high season, our live lobsters all come from the Atlantic ... Lobster meat is low in calories and an excellent source of protein and omega 3.

Salmon

This whole fillet of fresh Atlantic salmon from aquaculture comes from Canada. Farmed salmon is a healthy and nutritious food ... It is a fatty fish particularly rich in omega-3, therefore beneficial for cardiovascular health. Plus, it's a good source of high-quality protein, vitamin D, and calcium. Health Canada also recommends consuming at least two servings of oily fish per week as part of a balanced diet.

Shrimp

Having a firm and crunchy texture, northern shrimp, better known as Matane shrimp, have a fine and delicate taste ... Low in fat, they are notably a source of protein, Omega-3, Omega-6 and vitamin B12. Its consumption is therefore excellent for heart health and for strengthening the immune system. However, the consumption of Nordic shrimp should be done in moderation since they are also high in cholesterol.

Tuna

rich in vitamin B12 and selenium, its red flesh is sweet and gives large flakes ... It is a perfect fish to taste in the summer with which you can make a ton of recipes, but especially tartars or sashimi.
