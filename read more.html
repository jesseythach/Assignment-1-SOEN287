The prime rib roast is the creme de la creme of roasts. Recognized for being tender and juicy, accompany it with ... with bran au jus for even more flavor. We suggest baking at 325°F or if you have more time, at 275°F. The roast will be very juicy, provided it does not exceed the internal temperature of 71°C (160°F), which is equivalent to a “medium” degree of doneness. (Internal temperature for a medium-rare: 63°C or 145°F.) To make your roast even better, let it rest for about twenty minutes in aluminum foil after cooking. This last step will make all the difference.
